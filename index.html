Reinforcement Learning Projects

Objective: Implement and evaluate various reinforcement learning algorithms and strategies in different problem settings, including bandit learning algorithms, grid world navigation, and policy optimization methods.

Technologies Used: Python, Jupyter Notebook, TensorFlow (for some visualizations and practices).

Role and Contributions: As part of a team of two, I focused on Part 2 of all three projects. My contributions included:

  • Implementing algorithms for non-stationary bandit problems, such as drift changes, mean-reverting changes, and abrupt changes, in the bandit learning experiments.
  • Applying Monte Carlo methods (with and without exploring starts) to learn optimal policies in a modified grid world problem, introducing terminal states and dynamic changes.
  • Designing and evaluating reinforcement learning policies through policy iteration and applying importance sampling in complex grid world scenarios.

Final Outcomes: Successfully developed and tested reinforcement learning algorithms that demonstrated effective learning strategies and policy optimizations. The projects contributed to a deeper understanding of how reinforcement learning can be applied to dynamic and complex environments, showcasing optimal solutions in various scenarios.
